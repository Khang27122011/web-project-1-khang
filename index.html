    • About KFC

    KFC KFC, short for Kentucky Fried Chicken, is one of the world’s largest and most popular fast-food chains specializing in fried chicken. Founded by Colonel Harland Sanders in 1952 in the United States, KFC is famous for its “Original Recipe” chicken, made with a secret blend of 11 herbs and spices. KFC's mission is to provide high-quality, delicious chicken with a unique flavor that keeps customers coming back. The brand has expanded globally with thousands of locations in over 150 countries, offering a variety of chicken dishes including fried chicken pieces, chicken sandwiches, wraps, and sides like coleslaw and mashed potatoes. KFC has become a cultural icon in the fast-food industry, known for its crispy, flavorful chicken and iconic red-and-white branding featuring Colonel Sanders.

    KFC has over 24,000 restaurants worldwide and continues to expand. It offers a variety of chicken meals to suit different tastes.
